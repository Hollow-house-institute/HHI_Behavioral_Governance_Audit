# Executive Governance Summary
## HHI Behavioral Governance Audit
### Multi-Agent Healthcare Coordination Simulation

## Executive Finding

The audit identified Governance Drift across a coordinated healthcare workflow involving:
- symptom assessment systems
- scheduling orchestration
- insurance verification
- pharmacy escalation signals
- human clinical oversight

The simulation demonstrates how distributed AI coordination can degrade Decision Boundary integrity during execution despite existing policy controls.

---

# Primary Governance Failures Identified

| Failure Pattern | Operational Impact |
|---|---|
| Escalation Suppression | Pharmacy risk signals failed to persist across systems |
| Decision Substitution | Scheduling optimization overrode escalation visibility |
| Accountability Diffusion | Authority ownership became fragmented across systems |
| Authority Drift | Human oversight retained nominal authority but incomplete context |
| Governance Drift | Runtime behavior diverged from documented oversight expectations |

---

# Why Existing Governance Failed

The environment relied primarily on:
- post-hoc review
- static workflow assumptions
- disconnected escalation logic
- incomplete interaction visibility

This created Governance Lag during active coordination between systems.

Human-in-the-Loop oversight existed formally but lacked complete execution-time visibility.

---

# HHI Governance Controls Applied

HHI introduced:
- Governance Telemetry
- Interaction Trace continuity
- Decision Boundary mapping
- Stop Authority visibility
- Continuous Assurance controls
- Longitudinal Accountability tracking

These controls exposed hidden coordination failures during runtime operation.
